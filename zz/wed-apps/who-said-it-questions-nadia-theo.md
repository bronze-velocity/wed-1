# "Who Said It?" — a better, funnier question set

A worked example of how to write **Who Said It?** texts that actually land in a
room. The whole game lives or dies on one thing: *specificity*. Generic couple
banter ("babe come home") gets a coin-flip guess and a polite laugh. A text that
could only have come from **this** relationship gets a gasp, then an argument at
the table, then someone yelling the answer before the reveal.

So before writing a single text, we define the couple.

---

## The couple (defined first — do this every time)

**Nadia & Theo.**

- **Nadia** — ER trauma nurse, permanent night shifts. Dark, fast, competitive to
  a clinical degree. Ran a marathon "to see if I could" and now won't stop
  mentioning it. Texts in lowercase, no punctuation, at 4am. Keeps grudges like
  receipts.
- **Theo** — high-school marine-biology teacher, obsessive home cook, sourdough
  guy. Calm to the point of being suspicious. Keeps a spreadsheet for everything,
  including "spreadsheet of spreadsheets." Texts in full sentences with correct
  apostrophes. Says "no worries" and means it, which infuriates Nadia.
- **How they met:** a scuba trip in Cozumel; she corrected the dive instructor,
  he defended the dive instructor, they did not speak for the rest of the boat
  ride and have not stopped since.
- **The cast of running bits** (this is the gold):
  - **Clint** — the sourdough starter. Theo talks about Clint like a dependent.
  - **Biscuit & Moose** — two rescue greyhounds who are afraid of the floor.
  - **The thermostat war** — Nadia is always hot, Theo is always cold.
  - **The spreadsheet** — Theo has logged things Nadia does not know are logged.
  - **Night shift** — half of Nadia's texts arrive while the room was asleep.

Notice: none of this is "a couple." It's *these two*. Every text below is only
funny because you now know Clint exists.

---

## How to read this file

Each text is tagged with:
- **Who** — the real sender (the answer).
- **Split** — roughly how the room guesses, and why that's the fun part.
- **MC note** — the one line the emcee reads out loud to detonate it.

Order them **easy → chaotic** so the room warms up before the hard ones.

---

## Round 1 — Warm-ups (the room should get these)

**1.** "i am not saying i could perform surgery on the dog. i am saying i've seen it done and i'm confident."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** ~85% correct. Perfect opener — everyone who's met her knows.
- **MC note:** "Biscuit and Moose have never been consulted about this plan."

**2.** "Update: Clint is thriving. I fed him at 6, he doubled by noon. Proud of our boy."
- **Who:** Theo
- **Split:** ~80% correct once the room learns Clint is bread.
- **MC note:** "For the four people who don't know — Clint is the sourdough starter."

**3.** "i will be home at 7am. do not talk to me until 11. i love you. do not talk to me."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** ~78%. The double "do not talk to me" is the tell.
- **MC note:** "This is the warmest text Nadia has ever sent before a coffee."

---

## Round 2 — The petty ones (family-vs-family bait)

**4.** "i checked the thermostat. it says 71. you moved it. i have proof. i took a photo of it at 68 at 9pm."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** near 50/50 — everyone assumes the spreadsheet guy keeps the photo
  evidence, but it's *her*.
- **MC note:** "The thermostat war has now entered its forensic phase."

**5.** "I've started a tab. You owe me 3 'you were rights' and one 'the map was correct.' I am not letting these expire."
- **Who:** Theo
- **Split:** ~60% guess Theo (the spreadsheet energy gives it away, but some
  swear Nadia keeps score harder).
- **MC note:** "Theo has, in fact, a spreadsheet for this. Tab: 'Vindication.'"

**6.** "you cannot 'no worries' me right now. i am worried. that is the entire situation."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** ~70%. The room loves this because Theo's "no worries" is legendary.
- **MC note:** "Theo, for the record, said 'no worries' immediately after this."

**7.** "For the last time, the dogs are not 'cold.' They are greyhounds. This is their whole personality."
- **Who:** Theo
- **Split:** ~55%. Trickier — Nadia is usually the one defending the dogs.
- **MC note:** "Biscuit is currently wearing a sweater. So the dogs won."

---

## Round 3 — The unhinged ones (the screenshots people scream at)

**8.** "so hypothetically if a patient came in having done the exact thing you did on the ladder yesterday, i would have judged them. openly."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** ~75%. Clinical contempt is her signature.
- **MC note:** "Theo is fine. The ladder is not. Ask him about the gutters later."

**9.** "I ran the numbers and if we skip one dinner out a week we can afford a slightly nicer scuba regulator by August. I made a chart. The chart is beautiful."
- **Who:** Theo
- **Split:** ~85%. "I made a chart. The chart is beautiful." could not be anyone else.
- **MC note:** "The chart was, by all accounts, beautiful."

**10.** "i told the entire night shift about cozumel and now three nurses want to know if the dive instructor is single. i said i'd ask you. i will not be asking you."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** ~65%. Callback to the origin story — the room eats this up.
- **MC note:** "This is how we all learned how Nadia and Theo met."

**11.** "Genuine question, no judgment: how many of the marathon medals are we displaying, and is the number going to keep going up."
- **Who:** Theo
- **Split:** near 50/50 — sounds like something either would say, but the dry
  restraint is pure Theo.
- **MC note:** "There is one medal. It is in the hallway. We see it daily."

---

## Round 4 — The soft ones (land the plane)

**12.** "i had a rough one tonight. lost someone. i'm okay. i just wanted to text you before i forgot what a good day feels like. see you at 7."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** the room goes quiet. Most guess her, and getting it right feels like
  a gift. Put this late — it re-centers the whole game.
- **MC note:** (Read it slowly. Don't rush to the next one.)

**13.** "I kept the boarding pass from Cozumel. It was in a book I hadn't opened since. I'm not going to make it weird, I just wanted you to know it exists."
- **Who:** Theo
- **Split:** ~70%. The restraint ("I'm not going to make it weird") is the tell.
- **MC note:** "It's framed now. He made it weird. We love that he made it weird."

**14.** "okay you win. the map was right. i have updated the tab. the tab is now zero. don't say anything."
- **Who:** Nadia
- **Split:** ~60%. The payoff to text #5 — if you used both, save this for last in
  the round for the callback laugh.
- **MC note:** "The tab reached zero exactly once. This is the recorded moment."

## The Spicy Round (passkey-locked — friends' tables only)

On the night these are behind a code; the parents' tables don't get it. Keep
them affectionate, never actually humiliating.

**S1.** "i'm not saying the honeymoon itinerary needs a spreadsheet. i'm saying if it had one, i would read it. once. and then ignore it lovingly."
- **Who:** Nadia · **MC note:** "The itinerary has a spreadsheet. Of course it does."

**S2.** "Whatever happens tonight, if I say 'no worries' at the altar, know that I have never meant it more and also that I'm terrified."
- **Who:** Theo · **MC note:** "He did, in fact, say it at the altar. You heard it."

## The rules this set follows (reusable checklist)

Steal this structure for any couple:

1. **Define the couple first.** Jobs, texting style, origin story, and 3–5
   *named* running bits (a pet, an object, a recurring fight). Named specifics
   are the entire trick.
2. **Give each person a distinct voice.** Here: Nadia = lowercase, no
   punctuation, 4am, blunt. Theo = full sentences, apostrophes, "no worries,"
   spreadsheets. The guessing is only fun when the voices are learnable.
3. **Build running gags across texts.** Clint, the thermostat, the tab, the map,
   Cozumel — callbacks make later texts hit harder and reward paying attention.
4. **Vary the emotional register.** Petty → unhinged → genuinely tender. One soft
   text late (#12) makes the whole room feel the thing under the jokes.
5. **Engineer the split.** The best texts are the ~50/50 ones where the room
   argues. Write a few where the "obvious" answer is wrong.
6. **Order easy → hard → tender.** Warm them up, let it get chaotic, land soft.
7. **Keep the spicy round kind.** Behind a passkey, but never a text that would
   actually embarrass someone in front of their grandmother.

## Sourcing these from a real couple (the intake)

Ask each partner, separately, for:
- 5 texts they've sent that the other would recognize instantly.
- The pet's / object's / inside-joke's name and its origin.
- The origin-story detail they *always* disagree on.
- One text that made them laugh and one that made them cry.
- The thing their partner does that they've secretly been keeping track of.

Then the couple approves the final set — same moderation model as every Wepho
app. Nothing hits the big screen they haven't blessed.
