# 20 Real-World Examples: Interactive Games & Apps at Weddings

Research compiled from wedding blogs, vendor case studies, and real-couple write-ups covering how people actually run games and phone/web apps with guests **before, during, and after** the wedding. Each entry notes what it is, how guests interact, and where it's documented.

*Compiled 2026-07-19. Sources listed at the bottom.*

---

## During the Reception

### 1. Live couple's trivia on the big screen (Kahoot / My Wedding Trivia)
Couples build 15–20 multiple-choice questions about their relationship ("who said 'I love you' first?", "where was the first date?"). A game PIN or QR code goes up on the projector; guests join from their phones with **no app download**, answer in real time, and a live leaderboard shows who knows the couple best. Blogs recommend running it during dinner or between buffet courses so seated guests have something to do.
*Sources: [Kahoot Wedding Games](https://kahoot.com/home/wedding-games/); [My Wedding Trivia](https://myweddingtrivia.com/); [The Knot trivia guide](https://www.theknot.com/content/wedding-trivia-questions).*

### 2. The Shoe Game (analog game, increasingly run with live polling)
The classic: couple sits back-to-back, each holds one of their own shoes and one of their partner's, and an emcee asks questions ("who's the better cook?", "who said I love you first?") — they answer by raising a shoe. Modern couples run a phone-based version where **guests also vote** on each question via Mentimeter and results appear on screen, turning it into an audience debate.
*Sources: [The Knot Shoe Game (102 questions)](https://www.theknot.com/content/shoe-game-wedding-reception-game-questions); [WeddingWire](https://www.weddingwire.com/wedding-ideas/wedding-shoe-game); [Mentimeter wedding game template](https://www.mentimeter.com/templates/p/private-wedding-game).*

### 3. "Who Said It?" / how-well-do-you-know-us guessing game
Quotes or facts are shown and guests guess which partner said/did it. Runs well as a Kahoot-style quiz or a Mentimeter poll on the big screen. Directly parallels the Wepho homepage demo concept.
*Sources: [Mentimeter wedding game](https://www.mentimeter.com/templates/p/private-wedding-game); [SlideLizard wedding quiz ideas](https://slidelizard.com/en/blog/wedding-quiz).*

### 4. Live photo wall / slideshow from guests' phones (Kululu, GuestCam, Wedibox)
Guests scan a QR code, snap or upload photos, and images appear **within seconds** on the venue projector as a rolling slideshow. Vendors report it measurably increases guest participation because people take more photos once they see their own show up on the wall. GuestCam adds face-recognition so guests upload a selfie and instantly find every photo they're in.
*Sources: [Kululu (4.7★, 500+ reviews)](https://www.kululu.com/wedding-photo-sharing-app); [GuestCam blog](https://guestcam.co/blog/best-wedding-photo-sharing-sites); [Wedibox slideshow feature](https://www.wedibox.com/features/wedding-slideshow); [Waldo for Weddings](https://waldophotos.com/weddings/).*

### 5. Selfie challenges with live voting (Selfiewall)
Guests complete photo prompts on their phones; every shot streams to the projector as a live slideshow, then guests **vote via the browser** for the best photo of the night, and the winner is crowned on screen. A structured, competitive layer on top of the basic photo wall.
*Source: [Selfiewall wedding games](https://www.selfiewall.net/en/wedding-games.php).*

### 6. Digital photo scavenger hunt
Guests get a checklist of moments to capture — "a group selfie," "the bride laughing," "grandma dancing," "someone crying happy tears," "the youngest person at your table." Photos upload straight to a shared gallery / photo wall via QR code, no app needed. Blogs suggest mixing ceremony moments, reception details, and guest-connection prompts, and announcing it at dinner.
*Sources: [Bananainvite games guide](https://www.bananainvite.com/blog/15-fun-wedding-games-for-guests-reception-ideas); [pix.wedding (40+ challenges)](https://www.pix.wedding/wedding-photo-scavenger-hunt-challenges-guests); [WedPicsQR scavenger hunt](https://www.wedpicsqr.com/blog/wedding-scavenger-hunt); [Scene disposable](https://scenedisposable.com/blog/photo-scavenger-hunt).*

### 7. QR-code DJ song requests with crowd voting (Weddie, Fotify, CeolCode)
A QR code on tables / near the DJ opens a browser form; guests submit a song in under 30 seconds, no login. The DJ sees one organized, legible list with **vote counts** showing which requests have the most crowd support, and gets push notifications on new submissions. Several DJ services (JAMMIN' DJs, CeolCode) document how they integrate this into real weddings.
*Sources: [Weddie music](https://www.weddie.app/en/music); [Fotify DJ song request](https://fotify.app/dj-song-request/); [CeolCode DJ guide](https://ceolcode.com/02-qr-code-song-requests-complete-dj-guide/); [JAMMIN' DJs Atlanta](https://www.jammindjs.net/atlanta-corporate-dj/how-to-integrate-qr-song-requests-with-your-atlanta-dj-wedding-the-right-way/).*

### 8. Live audience polls on the big screen (Mentimeter / Poll Everywhere)
Beyond trivia — light-hearted "this or that" votes ("who's the better driver?", "beach or mountains honeymoon?") or letting guests **vote for the first-dance song** live. Results form on the shared screen in real time as votes land. Also used for a "best toast" contest where guests vote for their favorite speech.
*Sources: [Mentimeter live polling](https://www.mentimeter.com/features/live-polling); [Bananainvite live poll](https://www.bananainvite.com/blog/15-fun-wedding-games-for-guests-reception-ideas); [Poll Everywhere audience tools](https://www.polleverywhere.com/use-cases/best-audience-response-tools-2026).*

### 9. Guest matchmaking / "Tinder for the reception" (Fotify Match & Connect)
Single guests privately swipe through other attendees during cocktail hour and the reception; when two people mutually like each other, their social handles are revealed. Fotify's blog frames it (at ~$20/wedding) as their highest-ROI reception add-on for getting strangers talking.
*Source: [Fotify modern reception games 2026](https://fotify.app/blog/modern-wedding-reception-games-2026/).*

### 10. Live social wall / hashtag feed (Flockler, Taggbox, Waldo)
A projector pulls in guest posts tagged with the wedding hashtag from Instagram/TikTok/etc., or from a direct QR upload link, and displays them within seconds. Vendors describe a "continuous cycle" — seeing posts on screen prompts more guests to post. Photographers are asked to post behind-the-scenes shots to seed participation.
*Sources: [Flockler social wall for weddings](https://flockler.com/blog/social-wall-weddings); [Taggbox hashtag wall](https://taggbox.com/social-wedding-hashtag-wall/); [Wedavo live slideshow](https://www.wedavo.com/en-US/blog/live-slideshow-at-the-wedding-how-it-works).*

### 11. Phone-based photo booth (QR → props → gallery)
A 2026 twist on the rented booth: guests scan a QR code, take photos with on-screen digital props/filters, and shots drop into the shared album and appear on venue screens in real time — no booth rental, no attendant.
*Source: [Fotify modern reception games 2026](https://fotify.app/blog/modern-wedding-reception-games-2026/).*

### 12. Reception emoji / virtual-rice interaction for in-room + remote guests (Lovecast)
Live-streamed weddings where guests — remote *and* in the room — react with text chat, emojis, and even "throw virtual rice" across the screen. Two-way interaction that keeps distant guests part of the party rather than passive viewers.
*Sources: [Lovecast](https://www.lovecastapp.com/); [LoveStream virtual wedding guide](https://www.lovestream.io/virtual-wedding-guide).*

## Before the Wedding

### 13. Interactive wedding-website guestbook / trivia teaser (Wedding Party app)
Couples turn their wedding website into a "virtual yearbook" — guests fill out fun trivia and profile prompts ahead of time, building an interactive digital guestbook before anyone arrives. A Practical Wedding documents five ways real couples used the Wedding Party app for pre-event engagement.
*Source: [A Practical Wedding — Wedding Party app](https://apracticalwedding.com/wedding-party-app/).*

### 14. Pre-collected video messages for guests who can't attend (Celebrate.buzz, Tribute)
Couples send a QR code / link ahead of time so friends and family — especially those who can't make it — record short video well-wishes in advance. Tribute's blog lists 20 real virtual-wedding approaches built around collecting and stitching these together before the day.
*Sources: [Celebrate.buzz video guestbook](https://www.celebrate.buzz/video-guestbook/wedding); [Tribute 20 virtual wedding ideas](https://blog.tribute.co/virtual-wedding-ideas/).*

### 15. Collaborative pre-wedding playlist building (Weddie)
Before the reception, guests are invited via link to suggest and up-vote songs, so the DJ walks in with a playlist the room actually wants. Weddie's blog covers real setups where the crowd co-builds the music beforehand.
*Source: [Weddie collaborative playlist blog](https://www.weddie.app/en/blog/wedding-playlist-guest-suggestions).*

### 16. Photo scavenger-hunt cards seeded at place settings
Not strictly an app, but the planning-side ritual: couples print challenge cards and place them at each setting *before* guests arrive (some pair them with disposable cameras for a tech-free version), then announce the game at dinner. Documented across multiple real-wedding guides as the setup that drives the highest participation.
*Sources: [pix.wedding](https://www.pix.wedding/wedding-photo-scavenger-hunt-challenges-guests); [WedPicsQR](https://www.wedpicsqr.com/blog/photo-scavenger-hunt-wedding); [Pinterest disposable-camera hunts](https://www.pinterest.com/ideas/photo-scavenger-hunt-wedding-disposable-camera/924096952248/).*

## After the Wedding

### 17. Post-event shared gallery + face recognition (GuestCam, Fotify)
After the party, everyone's uploads live in one gallery. GuestCam's face-recognition lets each guest submit a selfie and pull only the photos they appear in. Real reviews (Guestpix) describe couples "seeing their day through the eyes of guests" and ending up with hundreds of candid shots they'd never have gotten otherwise.
*Sources: [Guestpix reviews](https://guestpix.com/weddings/); [GuestCam best photo-sharing sites](https://guestcam.co/blog/best-wedding-photo-sharing-sites); [Fotify photo-sharing app](https://fotify.app/wedding-photo-sharing-app/).*

### 18. Digital guestbook keepsake compiled afterward (Kululu, Wedibox, ReplayMyDay)
Text, photos, voice notes, and video wishes collected via QR code get compiled into a downloadable digital keepsake the couple reviews after the honeymoon. Wedibox lets couples toggle which formats guests can submit; ReplayMyDay auto-assembles the book and even ran a live slideshow during the event.
*Sources: [Wedibox digital guestbook](https://www.wedibox.com/features/digital-guestbook); [Kululu digital guestbook](https://www.kululu.com/digital-wedding-guestbook); [ReplayMyDay](https://www.replaymyday.com/digital-guestbook-for-weddings).*

### 19. Auto-compiled highlight recap from guest content (ReplayMyDay / Waldo)
Guest-uploaded photos and clips get automatically assembled into a recap/slideshow the couple can share back to everyone after the wedding — closing the loop on all the content the crowd captured.
*Sources: [ReplayMyDay](https://www.replaymyday.com/digital-guestbook-for-weddings); [Waldo for Weddings](https://waldophotos.com/weddings/).*

### 20. Post-wedding "best photo" voting / awards
The competitive photo layer extended past the night: guests (or the couple) vote in the shared gallery for the best guest photo, funniest moment, etc., after the event. Selfiewall and similar tools carry the voting mechanic beyond the reception into the recap phase.
*Sources: [Selfiewall](https://www.selfiewall.net/en/wedding-games.php); [Bananainvite live poll voting](https://www.bananainvite.com/blog/15-fun-wedding-games-for-guests-reception-ideas).*

## Cross-Cutting Patterns Worth Noting (for Wepho positioning)

- **No app download** is the universal expectation — every credible tool runs in the phone browser via a QR code or short link. Anything requiring an install is treated as a non-starter.
- **The big screen is the multiplier.** Tools that surface guest activity on a projector (photo walls, live polls, slideshows) consistently report higher participation than tools that stay private on phones. Seeing your own contribution appear publicly is the hook.
- **Timing is deliberate.** Blogs repeatedly recommend launching interactive games during dinner / between buffet courses when guests are seated, bored, and reachable.
- **Off-the-shelf tools are generic.** Every example above is a template couples fill in — Kahoot, Mentimeter, a QR photo wall. None of them *know the couple*. That's exactly the gap Wepho's custom, specificity-driven apps are built to fill.
