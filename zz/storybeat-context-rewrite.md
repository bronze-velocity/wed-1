# StoryBeat Rewrites — Give a Cold Visitor Context

The homepage StoryBeats (`components/sections/StoryBeat1.js`, `StoryBeat2.js`) each
cold-open into a vivid present-tense scene, then link to an app page. That's evocative —
but a **first-time visitor doesn't get it.**

## What's actually wrong

Read StoryBeat1 as someone who landed here 8 seconds ago:

> *"Fifteen questions in, grandma is beating the college roommates at a game about the couple."*

You can't tell:
- **What is this?** A physical game? A printed quiz? An app? (It's a phone web app — but nothing says so until the tiny link label at the very bottom.)
- **How does it work?** Where do the questions come from, what do guests do, what's on the big screen?
- **What am I buying?** Is this a product, a service, a download?

The scene assumes you already read the app page. A cold visitor hasn't. The vividness lands
as *vague* because there's no frame around it.

## The fix (structural)

Every beat gets three things the current ones lack, in this order:

1. **An orienting eyebrow** — one short line, above the headline, that names *what it is* before the scene starts. (e.g. `A live phone game · plays during dinner`) This is the single biggest fix.
2. **A headline** — keep the vivid scene, but make sure it reads as a *moment happening on screens/phones*, not an abstract event.
3. **A grounded description** — name the mechanic plainly: guests scan a QR code (no app to download), do X on their phones, Y happens on the big screen, and the couple keeps Z.

Below are **4 ready-to-use options**, each a different vibe. Pick any two for the two slots
(the page is built on a laugh/cry contrast — so pair one light option with one emotional one).

---

## Option 1 — Couple Trivia (#1) · vibe: competitive / laugh

- **slug:** `couple-trivia`
- **eyebrow:** `A live trivia game show · the whole room plays together, host on the mic`
- **headline:** **Fifteen questions in, the whole room is shouting at the same answer — and grandma is beating the college roommates.**
- **description:** A host picks up the mic and one live game begins for the entire room at once — not a self-paced quiz each table plays alone. Guests scan a QR code once (nothing to download), then every phone gets the same question at the same moment. A shared countdown moves everyone together, the leaderboard climbs on the big screen between rounds — how you actually met, who said "I love you" first, what she said when he proposed — and the winner heads to the mic for a toast. You write the questions in about twenty minutes; we build the rest.
- **link label:** `Live "How Well Do You Know Us?" Trivia — App #1`

---

## Option 2 — Anniversary Time Capsule (#3) · vibe: emotional / keepsake

- **slug:** `anniversary-time-capsule`
- **eyebrow:** `A sealed video keepsake · recorded on your phones, opened years later`
- **headline:** **Your grandmother records forty-five seconds of advice. It stays sealed until your tenth anniversary.**
- **description:** During cocktail hour, guests record a short video to your future selves — straight from their phone's browser, no app, no account. The capsule seals; no one can open it early, not even you. Years from now an unlock link arrives, and you watch every message together for the first time — including the ones from people who might not be there when you do.
- **link label:** `Anniversary Time Capsule — App #3`

## Option 3 — Custom Wedding Bingo (#11) · vibe: playful / all-night energy

- **slug:** `wedding-bingo`
- **eyebrow:** `A phone bingo card · a different one for every guest, running all night`
- **headline:** **The DJ plays ABBA and your aunt gasps — she's one square from bingo.**
- **description:** Every guest opens a different card on their phone (scan once, no download) and the squares are all yours — "best man cries during his own toast," "someone brings up the Croatia trip," "flower girl refuses to walk." They tap a square the second it happens; when someone finally yells bingo, their card flashes up on the big screen. You write the 40–60 squares — the room roasts itself all night.
- **link label:** `Custom Wedding Bingo — App #11`

## Option 4 — Unpopular Opinions (#9) · vibe: mingle / icebreaker

- **slug:** `unpopular-opinions`
- **eyebrow:** `A live vote · breaks the ice between tables of strangers`
- **headline:** **Two strangers at table six are arguing about pineapple on pizza before the drinks run out.**
- **description:** You pre-load a dozen of your realest takes — pineapple on pizza, mornings vs. nights, whether *The Office* is overrated — with your actual stance on each. Guests scan a QR code and vote agree or disagree from their phones, and the split shows live on the big screen. It turns a room of people who've never met into a room that's mid-debate by the second opinion. Fifteen minutes of setup from you.
- **link label:** `The Unpopular Opinions Icebreaker — App #9`

# More options — the rest of the live catalog

Full treatments for the other non-`skip` apps (the two homepage demos — Love Letter Machine
and Who Said It — are excluded, since they already have their own demo sections).

**One thing the Couple Trivia note taught us:** a cold visitor also can't tell the *social
texture* of a beat — is the whole room doing this together at once, or is each guest off on
their own phone? Some apps are deliberately synced (one big-screen moment, everyone on the
same beat); others are deliberately self-paced (roam, record, or reflect alone) and that's
their strength. Each option below is tagged so you pick the texture on purpose. **Format**
is the honest label; don't write a self-paced app as if the room moves together.

---

## Option 5 — Venue Scavenger Hunt (#2) · vibe: romantic / explore

- **format:** self-paced by design — guests roam and scan on their own; the exploring *is* the point
- **slug:** `venue-scavenger-hunt`
- **eyebrow:** `A hidden-QR hunt · guests explore the venue at their own pace during cocktail hour`
- **headline:** **The QR code behind the photo wall just told your cousin how you actually met.**
- **description:** Hidden around the venue are QR codes, each unlocking one chapter of your love story — the first date, the trip where it got serious, the proposal — in your own words and photos. Guests wander and scan (nothing to download), piecing it together in little groups, and the hardest-to-find last code reveals a private message from the two of you. Meant to be roamed, not marched through in sync.
- **link label:** `Venue Scavenger Hunt — App #2`

---

## Option 6 — Future Home Map (#14) · vibe: get-them-talking / live map

- **format:** whole-room, ambient — one live map on the big screen that everyone feeds all night
- **slug:** `future-home-map`
- **eyebrow:** `A live map on the big screen · fills up with guest pins all through dinner`
- **headline:** **Lisbon has twelve pins now, and two tables are arguing about whether you should actually move there.**
- **description:** The map starts with a few pins you dropped — your apartment, the spot of your first date, where he proposed — and fills up live on the big screen as guests add their own from their phones (no download): a restaurant a friend swears by, the neighborhood your roommate insists you'd love, a park for the kids nobody's mentioned yet. By the end of the night your future is a dense, opinionated map — and half of it ends up starred in your real one by morning. Yours to keep as a printed poster.
- **link label:** `Design Our Future Home Map — App #14`

---

## Option 7 — Prediction Vault (#6) · vibe: keepsake / opened years later

- **format:** submit during the night, reveal on your anniversaries — no big-screen moment
- **slug:** `prediction-vault`
- **eyebrow:** `Sealed guest predictions · unlocked one anniversary at a time, for decades`
- **headline:** **Your best man bet you'll still be arguing about the thermostat in 2040 — with his name on it.**
- **description:** Guests predict your future from their phones — first kid, where you'll live, what you'll still be bickering about — and sort each bet by how far out they're wagering: 1, 5, 10, or 25 years. The vault seals at midnight (nothing to download, nothing to open early). Then every anniversary, that year's batch unlocks and you score them together — a wedding gift that keeps arriving, one anniversary at a time.
- **link label:** `The Prediction Vault — App #6`

---

## Option 8 — Advice Oracle (#12) · vibe: keepsake / live wall

- **format:** whole-room, ambient — answers scroll onto the wall as they come in, plus an optional live-question moment
- **slug:** `advice-oracle`
- **eyebrow:** `A live advice wall · real answers scroll up as guests write them`
- **headline:** **You post "we can't agree on where to live — help," and a hundred people answer at once.**
- **description:** Instead of a guest book no one reopens, structured prompts land on guests' phones — best advice for a long marriage, a mistake to avoid, something they wish they'd known — and the answers stream onto the big screen in real time, some funny, some clearly hard-won. You can even post a live question of your own mid-reception and watch the room respond like a hundred people just became your advisors. Every answer is saved into a "Book of Advice" you'll actually reread.
- **link label:** `The Advice Oracle — App #12`

## Option 9 — Story Chain (#22) · vibe: playful chaos / the room writes together

- **format:** whole-room, synced — one story builds live on the big screen, everyone watching the same line land
- **slug:** `story-chain`
- **eyebrow:** `A live story on the big screen · the whole room writes it, one sentence each`
- **headline:** **By dessert your college friends have turned your love story into a two-page myth that's almost entirely untrue.**
- **description:** One opening line sits on the big screen — "Their love story actually began when…" — and guests add a sentence at a time from their phones (no download), each new line appearing live for the room to react to. It lurches from sweet to absurd and back, the DJ reads the best lines aloud, and a short cooldown keeps anyone from hijacking the plot. A week later it arrives as a small printed chapbook: your wedding's official, wildly inaccurate origin story.
- **link label:** `The Story Chain — App #22`

---

## Option 10 — Ask Us Anything (#23) · vibe: live Q&A / synced at the mic

- **format:** whole-room, synced — guests vote questions up, you answer the top ten live at the mic
- **slug:** `ask-us-anything`
- **eyebrow:** `A live Q&A · guests vote up questions, you answer the top ten at the mic`
- **headline:** **The room voted your honest answer about kids above the zombie-apocalypse question.**
- **description:** During dinner, guests submit questions from their phones and upvote the ones they actually want answered (no download) — the good ones climb, the safe ones sink. By dessert there's a clear top ten on the big screen, and you take the mic to answer the questions your guests genuinely chose, not the ones a host guessed at. Some land as jokes. One or two land somewhere quieter. You keep the full ranked list after.
- **link label:** `Ask Us Anything — App #23`

---

## Option 11 — Video Guestbook (#24) · vibe: emotional / keepsake

- **format:** self-paced by design — each guest records privately from their seat; nothing on the wall
- **slug:** `video-guestbook`
- **eyebrow:** `A video guestbook · each guest records 60 seconds from their seat — no booth, no line`
- **headline:** **No booth, no line — your grandmother just recorded sixty seconds you'll replay for the rest of your life.**
- **description:** Instead of signing a book, each guest opens a prompt on their phone — "What do you want them to remember?" — and records a short video from wherever they're sitting (no app, no queue, a discreet re-record button so nobody submits a bad take). A week later a curated film arrives, and every individual clip is archived on its own too — so decades from now, every voice is still there, one by one.
- **link label:** `The Video Guestbook — App #24`

## Recommended pairing

The page is built on a laugh/cry contrast, so pair one light/energetic beat with one
emotional one — and, ideally, one *synced* whole-room moment with one *self-paced or
reveal-later* keepsake, so the two beats don't feel like the same texture twice.

**Default: Option 1 (Trivia, synced/laugh) + Option 2 (Time Capsule, reveal-later/cry)** —
keeps the contrast the page is built around, both are chosen (non-`skip`) apps, and both now
tell a cold visitor exactly what they're looking at before the scene plays.

Other strong pairs: **Option 9 (Story Chain) + Option 11 (Video Guestbook)** for a
writer-ish couple; **Option 10 (Ask Us Anything) + Option 6 (Prediction Vault)** to lead
with a live-mic moment and close on a decades-long keepsake.
