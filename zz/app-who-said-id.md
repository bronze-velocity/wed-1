## 14. Who Said It?

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Stop the room
**Moments:** Dinner

### The couple this is for
Jack and Simone met on Hinge, but they had a 4-month text conversation before they ever met in person. They still screenshot each other's texts and send them back at strategic moments.

### What the app does
The screen shows a real text from Jack or Simone's phone history — with the sender's name blurred. Guests vote on their phone: *Jack or Simone?* Points for correct answers, humiliation for wrong ones. Some texts are early-dating flirty, some are just *"we're out of oat milk again."*

### Cool features
- 20 texts across their relationship, chronologically ordered so guests watch the couple's voice change
- Bonus rounds: "guess the year" for extra points
- Optional "spicy round" that requires a special code shared at the friends' tables
- A running "who knows them better" scoreboard split between his family, her family, and mutual friends

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
Everyone's looking at the same screen and laughing at the same time. Phone use is one tap, then look up. It's 10 minutes of collective *"how did they end up together, honestly."*

### Non-app version
Print the texts on giant cards, MOH reveals one at a time, tables shout "Jack!" or "Simone!" and self-score.
- **Needed?** No — a card-based reveal is very close to as fun.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally — real-time voting and family-vs-family scoring are neat, but the core comedy is in the texts themselves, not the tech.
