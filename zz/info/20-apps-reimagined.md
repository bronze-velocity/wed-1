# 20 Custom Wedding Apps — Reimagined

Each app is built from scratch for one couple, used for one night, then archived. The subtext of every app on this list: *generic tools don't know who you are.*

**Five rules every app on this list respects:**
1. **Phone in, phone out in under 60 seconds.** No scrolling, no feeds, no lingering. Guests interact, then look up.
2. **The magic happens in the room, not on the screen.** Screens are triggers for real-world moments — a toast, a hug, a burst of laughter across the table.
3. **The couple curates.** Nothing goes public without their approval (or a trusted friend's). No moderation panic, no awkward surprises.
4. **The app is built for one couple, one night.** Nothing off-the-shelf. The couple brainstorms with us and can come up with any idea — however weird, however specific, however tied to their private history. If it fits their story, we build it.
5. **They bring the idea (or pick one of ours) — we build it beautifully.** The couple doesn't touch code, wireframes, or config. We take the concept and ship it with a seamless user experience, design that matches their wedding's visual language, and rock-solid live-night reliability.

Classifications:
- **Vibes:** Make them laugh · Make them cry · Get them talking · Create a keepsake · Stop the room
- **Moments:** Arrival · Cocktail Hour · Dinner · Speeches · Dancing · All Day

## 1. The Love Letter Machine

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** Cocktail Hour · Dinner

### The couple this is for
Priya and Ben met in 2019 and spent 14 months of the pandemic 3,000 miles apart. They wrote each other one handwritten letter every week. They still have all 61 letters in a shoebox under their bed.

### What the app does
Guests type a short letter to the couple on their phone. It's not a "message" — the UI literally looks like Priya's handwriting on cream paper, complete with the little heart she draws next to Ben's name. Guest hits send. Priya's maid of honor (pre-briefed) sees it on a tablet, taps approve. The letter appears on the big screen for 20 seconds — typed out one word at a time, in that same handwriting, like it's being written in real time. Then it's gone.

### Cool features
- Guest chooses one of three "stationery" styles (Priya's handwriting, Ben's handwriting, or a shared typewriter font)
- One-tap prompts if the guest freezes: *"Tell them about the first time you met one of them" / "What did you think when you met the other?"*
- At end of night, all 100+ letters compiled into a real hardbound book, mailed to the couple two weeks later
- The maid of honor's tablet also lets her pin 3 letters as "read at the anniversary" — the couple gets those emailed on their 1-year anniversary

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
Guests spend 90 seconds on their phone once. The rest of the night they're watching the screen with everyone else — and every time a new letter appears, half the room stops talking to read it. It creates group hush moments, not individual scroll moments.

### Non-app version
Stack of cream cards and pens on every table, a decorated wooden box to drop them in, MOH reads a handful aloud between courses.
- **Needed?** No — cards work.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — the live handwritten-reveal on screen is a genuinely new emotional beat cards can't produce.

## 2. How We Actually Met (The Trivia Version)

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Get them talking
**Moments:** Cocktail Hour · Dinner

### The couple this is for
Jules and Cam are competitive about literally everything — board game nights end in shouting, they have a rolling Pop-A-Shot score going back to 2021, and their pre-wedding Google Doc has a tab called "Wedding Bets."

### What the app does
Guests join a live trivia game, but every question is *specifically* about Jules and Cam. Not "when's the couple's anniversary" — it's *"Cam texted Jules first on the dating app. What was the opening line?"* with four brutally specific options including the real (and mildly embarrassing) one.

### Cool features
- 15 questions, 20 seconds each — the whole thing takes 8 minutes
- Live leaderboard on the big screen with guests' actual names, not usernames
- Table-level scoring on top of individual scoring, so the head table (parents + siblings) battles the college friends table
- Cam and Jules pre-recorded 5-second video reactions for each answer reveal — the *"nope, that was actually a Bumble opener from October 2020"* video plays after the wrong answer
- Winner gets to give an unscheduled 60-second toast

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
Trivia forces the room to focus on the same thing at the same time. No one's on their phone alone — they're screaming at their table. It's 8 minutes, not 80.

### Non-app version
Printed trivia sheets at each table, host reads questions from a mic, tables score themselves on paper.
- **Needed?** No — pub-quiz style works.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — live leaderboards, video reactions, and per-guest scoring turn it from a party game into a spectacle.

## 3. Guess the Year

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** Arrival · Cocktail Hour

### The couple this is for
Amara and Josh have been together since 9th grade — 14 years. Their friend group has photographic evidence of every haircut, every phase, every regrettable outfit.

### What the app does
As guests arrive, they see photos of Amara and Josh appear on lobby screens. For each photo, they guess the year on their phone. Their guess is compared to the actual year and they get a score. The photo stays on screen just long enough for the room to laugh at Josh's 2013 fedora.

### Cool features
- 40 photos across their 14 years — one every 90 seconds during cocktail hour
- Bonus rounds: *"What color was Amara's hair in this photo?"* (she went through 6)
- "Whose ex is this in the background?" special edition for their college friends only (unlocked by a code shared at their table)
- Final "you know them best" scoreboard at the end of cocktail hour — top 3 get a printed polaroid trophy
- The full set of 40 photos becomes a scrolling montage that plays during dinner

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It's ambient — guests glance at the screen, tap once, go back to their drink. It's the opposite of a phone hole. And the screens create shared laughter across strangers who don't know each other yet.

### Non-app version
Framed printed photos with a hidden year card on the back; guests wander and guess out loud during cocktail hour.
- **Needed?** No — a photo wall is a classic.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally — scoring and the montage payoff are livelier, but a good photo wall gets most of the way there.

## 4. Songs With Stories

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** All Day (peaks during Dinner and Dancing)

### The couple this is for
Emma and Dev met at a Phoebe Bridgers concert. They have shared Spotify playlists dating back to their first month together. Dev proposed by making Emma a playlist that spelled out "will you marry me" in the first letter of each song title.

### What the app does
Guests request a song for the dance floor — but they can't just request it. They have to write one sentence about why. *"Play 'Landslide' because it was playing at Dev's 30th birthday when Emma told me she was going to marry him."* When the song plays, the sentence appears on the big screen for the length of the intro.

### Cool features
- Pre-loaded with the couple's "must play" and "do not play" lists — guests see instantly if their song is unavailable
- DJ has a curator view that lets him accept/reject requests and reorder them
- Each song's "story" is timestamped and saved to a keepsake playlist Emma and Dev can replay on any anniversary
- Special "first dance dedication" slot — guests can submit a dedication that will be read *before* the first dance, and the couple picks one anonymously

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
Guests are motivated to actually dance to their song when it plays, because *their* sentence is on the screen. The dance floor stays full, not empty because everyone's requesting songs and then not dancing.

### Non-app version
Request slips at the DJ booth with a "why?" line; DJ reads the reason on the mic before dropping the song.
- **Needed?** No — slips + mic covers it.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — the on-screen sentence during the intro, the keepsake playlist, and the do-not-play filter are meaningfully better than paper.

## 5. The Prediction Booth

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** Cocktail Hour · Dinner

### The couple this is for
Rae and Tomás are 33, both scientists, both way too into probability. They have a shared spreadsheet called "Life Bets" with 47 open wagers, including "how many years until we own a house" and "who gets a gray hair first."

### What the app does
Guests answer specific prediction questions about the couple's future: *"In what year will they buy their first house?"* / *"How many kids?"* / *"Which of them will run a marathon first?"* / *"Will Tomás finally learn to cook risotto by their 5th anniversary?"* The predictions are sealed, aggregated, and delivered to the couple every year on their anniversary as a scheduled email.

### Cool features
- Weighted questions — some worth more points if you get them right
- Live histogram on screen during dinner: *"73% of you think they'll have kids within 3 years. Rae would like the room to know she disagrees."*
- 5-year, 10-year, 25-year time capsule tiers — you pick when your prediction gets revealed
- Guest gets a personalized "prediction receipt" texted to them so they remember what they bet on

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It's not a live entertainment loop — it's a 3-minute submission during cocktail hour, then it disappears. But it keeps giving back to the couple for decades.

### Non-app version
Prediction cards at each table, sealed in a jar labelled "Open in 5 years."
- **Needed?** No — a jar of predictions is a lovely low-tech classic.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — the live histogram ("73% think kids within 3 years"), scheduled anniversary emails, and multi-decade tiers are things a jar cannot do.

## 6. Toasts From Everywhere

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** Speeches · All Day

### The couple this is for
Nina and Chris have family across 4 continents. Nina's grandmother in Lagos couldn't fly. Chris's uncle in Melbourne is 82. Half the people they love aren't in the room.

### What the app does
Two weeks before the wedding, every invited guest — whether they RSVP'd yes or no — got a link. They record a 30-second video toast on their phone. All 180 toasts are curated into a 4-minute reel by an editor, screened right before the best man's speech. But the app doesn't stop there: during the reception, absent guests can send *live* short messages that stream on a "wall of love" screen in the lobby.

### Cool features
- Auto-generated captions in the language the guest speaks — Nina's grandmother speaks Yoruba, and the caption appears in English underneath
- Editor's dashboard for the maid of honor to pick highlight clips
- Absent guests can send a live emoji-reaction during the ceremony livestream — the reactions appear as ambient floating hearts on a small side screen
- Every toast is saved and delivered as a private YouTube playlist to the couple

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
The screen event is 4 minutes — a curated film, not a live feed. The rest of the wedding, the app is invisible. Absent-family features stay in the lobby where they belong.

### Non-app version
Ask absent guests to email a video to the MOH; she cuts a reel in iMovie and projects it. WhatsApp group for live absent-guest messages.
- **Needed?** No — email + WhatsApp gets you most of the way.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — auto-captioning across languages, the live ambient hearts, and the polished "wall of love" lobby moment are hard to DIY without them feeling janky.

## 7. Where We Know You From

**Vibes:** Get them talking · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** Arrival · Cocktail Hour

### The couple this is for
Lia and Sam have collected friends everywhere: Sam's Peace Corps posting in Guatemala, Lia's semester in Berlin, their post-college years in San Francisco, their current life in Brooklyn. Half the guests don't know each other. Small talk is going to be *hard.*

### What the app does
Every guest gets a link on their escort card. They fill out a 30-second profile: where they know Lia or Sam from, one thing they'd want a stranger at this wedding to ask them, and one thing they refuse to talk about (politely worded). During cocktail hour, they can look up any other guest at the wedding, or hit "surprise me" to be introduced to someone with something in common.

### Cool features
- The big screen shows a live map of the world with pins for where every guest knows the couple from — the pins pulse when a new guest checks in
- *"You and Marcus both spent time in Antigua"* prompts help strangers actually start conversations
- Optional "conversation starters" a guest can send anonymously to their table — *"Ask table 4 who's the loudest snorer here, and prepare for chaos"*
- Post-wedding, every guest gets a booklet of everyone they met, with the profiles they'd shared

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
The app *causes* IRL conversation. Guests aren't on it during dinner — they used it once during cocktail hour, and now they have a reason to talk to the stranger across the table.

### Non-app version
Escort cards with a "how I know the couple" line and a conversation-starter question. Big printed world map with pins guests place themselves.
- **Needed?** No — printed profiles and a physical pin map are charming.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — "surprise me" matching and the searchable directory create introductions that printed cards can't broker mid-conversation.

## 8. Mad Libs, But It's Their Vows

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Stop the room
**Moments:** Speeches

### The couple this is for
Kate and Jordan are the friends who make everyone snort-laugh. Kate does bits. Jordan has a legitimate side career on TikTok doing improv. Their engagement announcement was a mock press release.

### What the app does
Right before speeches, the DJ says "we need your help." Guests are prompted for words — noun, adjective, embarrassing verb, favorite food, celebrity name. When they hit submit, the words get randomly assembled into a fake "surprise vow renewal" script that Kate and Jordan then perform live at the mic, reading it for the first time.

### Cool features
- Word-frequency filters (no repeats, no boring nouns)
- The app tags submissions to a specific guest, so the couple can read at the end *"the word 'ferret' was contributed by Uncle Doug, who I always suspected"*
- Live audience voting on which of 3 word-sets Kate and Jordan should perform
- Full script printed as a scroll and handed to a random kid at the end to "carry into the future"

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It's a 4-minute segment during speeches. Guests contribute for 30 seconds, then everyone watches the same thing together. The screen and phone become a single shared performance.

### Non-app version
Slips of paper passed around asking for a noun/verb/etc.; MOH collects them in a hat and hands the couple a hand-filled script.
- **Needed?** No — mad libs is a paper game at its core.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — instant assembly, live audience voting between three variants, and per-guest attribution make it a real production instead of a slow paper-shuffle.

## 9. Advice for the Long Haul

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Create a keepsake · Get them talking
**Moments:** Dinner · All Day

### The couple this is for
Marisol and Dean are getting married later in life — she's 44, he's 47, both were married before. Half the room has been married 20+ years. There's a lot of wisdom sitting at those tables.

### What the app does
Guests answer one of five prompts: *"One thing I know now that I wish I knew at my wedding" / "The hardest year of our marriage was ___ because ___, and what saved it was ___" / "What no one tells you about year 10" / "When you're arguing at 11pm, remember ___" / "The best thing about being married for a long time is ___".* Responses are shown on a rotating side screen (with permission) and compiled into a printed book.

### Cool features
- Anonymous mode toggle — some advice is more honest when it's not signed
- Long-form allowed (up to 500 characters) because this crowd has things to say
- Prompts tagged by "years married" — the app knows to ask 5-year-marrieds different questions than 40-year-marrieds
- The couple gets a private hardcover of *only* the advice signed by name, plus a second copy with all the anonymous stuff

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It's ambient — guests fill it out when they naturally reach for their phone (waiting for dinner, in line for the bar). Nobody has to do it. The screen presence is subtle.

### Non-app version
Prompt cards on every table with an advice book to write in — a well-established wedding tradition.
- **Needed?** No — the advice book is a genre standard.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally — anonymous mode and years-married tagging pull more honest wisdom, but a book already does 90% of the job well.

## 10. Story Chain

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** Cocktail Hour · Dinner

### The couple this is for
Sabrina and Aidan are writers — she's a novelist, he's a screenwriter. Their first date was a 6-hour argument about the ending of *No Country for Old Men.*

### What the app does
The screen shows one opening sentence: *"Sabrina and Aidan's love story actually began when..."*. Guests take turns adding one sentence to the chain. Each new sentence appears on the big screen and the story grows in real time. The couple's writer friends will absolutely try to sabotage it. Chaos is the feature.

### Cool features
- 30-second cooldown between sentences per guest (no monopolizing)
- Sabrina's editor friend has a moderator button to redirect the story if it goes too far off the rails
- Multiple parallel "chapters" — one wholesome chain, one absurd chain, guests choose
- Final story printed as a chapbook the next morning, delivered to the honeymoon suite

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It's collaborative and public — every new sentence gets read aloud by the DJ if it's especially good. Guests are watching the screen and shouting suggestions, not scrolling.

### Non-app version
A long roll of paper on a table, one pen, guests add a sentence in turn like a party exquisite-corpse.
- **Needed?** No — the paper-scroll version is more tactile.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — the live big-screen reveal, cooldown enforcement, and parallel chapters keep the game moving and public in a way one pen can't.

## 11. Scavenger Hunt

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Get them talking
**Moments:** All Day

### The couple this is for
Callie and Wes met on a hiking trip. Their engagement was a scavenger hunt Wes built around Boston. They think weddings that force everyone to sit still are miserable.

### What the app does
Every guest gets a list of 20 photo missions on their phone. *"Photo of a stranger doing your grandma's signature move" / "The person here who's traveled furthest holding a slice of cake" / "Selfie with someone whose first name starts with the same letter as yours."* Complete missions, earn points, unlock levels. Every photo gets automatically added to the wedding album.

### Cool features
- Progressive difficulty — early missions are easy warm-ups, later ones require actual social effort
- Team mode for guests who came alone — auto-paired with someone at random
- The couple's inside-joke missions are hidden and only unlockable by their college friends who know the reference
- Real-time top-3 leaderboard on a small screen at the bar
- Full photo dump becomes the couple's shared album — probably better than the photographer's for candids

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
The whole app is a *pretext for IRL interaction.* You literally can't complete the missions without talking to people you don't know. Phones are used briefly, then put away for the interaction itself.

### Non-app version
Printed mission checklist per guest with disposable cameras or Polaroids to capture proof.
- **Needed?** No — a printed bingo card with disposables is a nostalgic classic.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — hidden inside-joke missions, live leaderboards, and the auto-compiled photo album are strictly better than paper checklists.

## 12. The Reaction Layer

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Stop the room
**Moments:** Speeches · Dancing

### The couple this is for
Nadia and Kai are both 28, both in tech, both grew up on Twitch chat and streamer culture. They know their wedding will be full of people trying not to be on their phones, and they'd rather channel that impulse than fight it.

### What the app does
During specific moments — the best man's speech, the first dance, the cake cutting — guests can send emoji reactions that appear as ambient bursts on the corners of the big screen. Not text. Not memes. Just a subtle stream of hearts, tears, laugh emojis, prayer hands. Like the moment has a soundtrack of emotional shorthand.

### Cool features
- Curated emoji set per moment — during the vows, only ❤️, 🥹, 😭; during the roast, unrestricted chaos
- Live "reaction density" heatmap — the best man can see when a joke landed and when it didn't
- Special "confetti burst" mode when a threshold of reactions hits at the same time (the room realizes it's cheering *together*)
- Post-wedding, an emoji timeline of the whole night — instantly a meme, but also weirdly touching

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
Emojis are ambient, not attention-grabbing. Guests tap, then look up. And it's only active during the 5 moments the couple designated — the rest of the night, the app is dormant.

### Non-app version
Cheering, laughter, sparklers, tossed napkins — the room's own reaction layer.
- **Needed?** No — the room already has one.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally, and risky — for a Twitch-culture couple it's fun, but for most weddings it substitutes screen-taps for the vocal reactions that would otherwise happen. Weakest concept on the list.

## 13. Recipe Box

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Create a keepsake · Get them talking
**Moments:** Dinner · All Day

### The couple this is for
Sofia and Miguel met in culinary school. Their first apartment had a wall calendar tracking what they cooked every night for a year. Their wedding menu was written by them, not the caterer.

### What the app does
Every guest submits one recipe — either "the dish I'd make for the couple to celebrate an anniversary" or "the thing my mother made me when I needed comfort." Photos welcomed. Everything gets compiled into a hardcover cookbook titled *"The Wedding Cookbook."* One guest a night for the rest of their lives.

### Cool features
- Voice-note recipes for guests who "don't measure" — auto-transcribed
- Sofia's mother's chef friend curates a "featured recipe of the night" that's actually incorporated into the reception's late-night snack menu (guests cheer when they realize what happened)
- The app suggests you pair your recipe with a story about the person who taught it to you
- After the wedding, the couple gets the cookbook AND a set of postcards — one recipe per postcard — to write thank-you notes on

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
Submission is opt-in and mostly happens during the natural "food" moments — after dinner, during dessert. Nothing on screen, no notifications. It's a quiet, tender app.

### Non-app version
Recipe cards in the invitation envelope; guests bring them filled in and drop them in a box at the wedding.
- **Needed?** No — recipe cards actually feel more heirloom than a phone form.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally — voice-note transcription for non-measuring cooks is a nice unlock, but the paper version might be *more* meaningful here. Consider recommending the paper hybrid.

## 14. Who Said It?

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Stop the room
**Moments:** Dinner

### The couple this is for
Jack and Simone met on Hinge, but they had a 4-month text conversation before they ever met in person. They still screenshot each other's texts and send them back at strategic moments.

### What the app does
The screen shows a real text from Jack or Simone's phone history — with the sender's name blurred. Guests vote on their phone: *Jack or Simone?* Points for correct answers, humiliation for wrong ones. Some texts are early-dating flirty, some are just *"we're out of oat milk again."*

### Cool features
- 20 texts across their relationship, chronologically ordered so guests watch the couple's voice change
- Bonus rounds: "guess the year" for extra points
- Optional "spicy round" that requires a special code shared at the friends' tables
- A running "who knows them better" scoreboard split between his family, her family, and mutual friends

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
Everyone's looking at the same screen and laughing at the same time. Phone use is one tap, then look up. It's 10 minutes of collective *"how did they end up together, honestly."*

### Non-app version
Print the texts on giant cards, MOH reveals one at a time, tables shout "Jack!" or "Simone!" and self-score.
- **Needed?** No — a card-based reveal is very close to as fun.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally — real-time voting and family-vs-family scoring are neat, but the core comedy is in the texts themselves, not the tech.

## 15. The Playlist Committee

**Vibes:** Get them talking
**Moments:** Dancing · All Day

### The couple this is for
Elena and Ruben have opinions about music. Big opinions. They already made a wedding playlist. But they know that as soon as the DJ plays *"Don't Stop Believin',"* their entire dance floor is going to riot in a good way, and they want that energy to be *earned.*

### What the app does
Guests don't request songs — they vote on categories the DJ presents live. *"Which decade next?" / "More slow, more fast, or throwback?" / "Which of these three: A, B, C?"*. Whichever wins, DJ plays. Real-time voting during dancing.

### Cool features
- Each guest gets 10 votes for the whole night — creates strategic voting
- Guests can spend 3 votes to trigger a "power request" — a specific song they want played, but it has to survive a majority vote
- Live leaderboard of "who's controlling the dance floor" — Uncle Rick becomes a folk hero when his 90s R&B run dominates for 20 minutes
- A "veto card" system for the couple — 3 vetoes for the whole night, and the reasons are printed on the veto card ("*Sorry, no 'Wonderwall,' Elena's ex played it too much*")

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
This is an app *for* dancing, not instead of dancing. You vote in 3 seconds and go back to the floor. It also makes the DJ set feel co-authored, so people actually dance to the songs they voted for.

### Non-app version
DJ shouts "who wants faster, who wants slower?" and reads the room.
- **Needed?** No — a good DJ already does this by feel.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — the vote-budget mechanic, veto cards, and "who's controlling the dance floor" leaderboard turn crowd-reading into an actual game. Genuinely novel.

## 16. Letters to the Future

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** All Day

### The couple this is for
Isabel and Rowan are both 29, both eldest siblings, both people who cry at Pixar movies. They want to remember tonight but they also know that in 20 years, tonight will feel very far away.

### What the app does
Guests write a short letter (or record a video) *to the couple in the future.* They pick when it gets delivered: 1 year, 5 years, 10 years, 25 years, 50 years. On those anniversaries, the couple gets a bundle of letters emailed to them from the past.

### Cool features
- The 50-year bundle includes letters from guests who are, statistically, unlikely to still be alive — that's part of the point
- Guests can attach a photo of themselves *from tonight* to their letter, so future-them sees present-them
- Special "for the kids" bundle — letters guests write for the couple's future children, delivered when the kids turn 18
- The couple can also write letters to *themselves* that the app delivers on the same schedule

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It's slow and thoughtful. Guests fill it out when they have a quiet moment. No screen presence, no interruptions. It's a gift the wedding gives the couple for decades.

### Non-app version
Envelopes labelled "Open in 1 year / 5 / 10 / 25" at each table; couple stores them in a keepsake box.
- **Needed?** No — envelopes-with-dates is a beautiful, tactile tradition.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes for the 25- and 50-year tiers where paper reliably gets lost. Scheduled delivery is the actual moat here.

## 17. Two-Minute Video Guestbook

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Create a keepsake
**Moments:** All Day (peaks after ceremony)

### The couple this is for
Beatriz and Owen have a huge family. Beatriz's dad died two years ago. She has a video of him from her college graduation and watches it every year on his birthday. She knows the exact value of a well-preserved video.

### What the app does
Instead of signing a book, guests record a 60-second video message on their phone. Simple prompt: *"What do you want them to remember?"* Auto-uploaded, curated by Owen's brother into a 20-minute film delivered the next week.

### Cool features
- Prompt cards to prevent freezing: *"tell them about a moment when you knew" / "advice from your longest marriage" / "the thing you love most about only one of them"*
- Discreet re-record button (no one has to submit a bad take)
- Grandparents and older guests get an "assisted mode" where their grandkid runs it for them
- The final film is delivered with individual clips also saved separately, so if someone in that film is no longer around in 30 years, the family has each one archived

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It replaces the guestbook — a thing everyone already does at weddings. It's not extra screen time; it's *substitution* of screen time for something more meaningful.

### Non-app version
Rented video-guestbook booth (there's a whole industry — AirBooth, After, etc.).
- **Needed?** Yes-ish — the concept exists as a booth rental, but a bespoke app version means every guest can record from their seat, on their own time, without a queue.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — no booth bottleneck, custom prompts, assisted mode for elders, and per-clip archival for future decades. Meaningful upgrade over rental booths.

## 18. Ask Us Anything

**Vibes:** Make them laugh · Get them talking · Stop the room
**Moments:** Dinner

### The couple this is for
Talia and Marco are the type who host dinner parties and then everyone stays until 2am arguing about ethics or which Beatle was actually the best. They love a Q&A. They love being on the spot.

### What the app does
Guests submit questions for the couple during dinner. Anonymous or credited. Ranges from silly *("which of you would survive longer in a zombie apocalypse")* to real *("what changed your mind about wanting kids").* Guests upvote questions they want answered. During dessert, the couple takes the mic and answers the top 10.

### Cool features
- Marco's brother has a moderator view to hide questions that are actively bad
- Categories: "Silly," "Sentimental," "Actually Kind of Deep," "Only Marco's Answer" so answers stay balanced
- Voting closes 15 minutes before dessert, results shown on screen with vote counts
- Best question wins the guest a small prize (a bottle of wine? a photo of the couple with a personal note?)

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
It's a bounded segment — 15 minutes of Q&A, then the app is dormant. And during those 15 minutes the entire room is watching the couple, not their phones.

### Non-app version
Index cards at each table, guests write questions, MOH shuffles and picks favorites.
- **Needed?** No — card-based AMAs are well-trodden.
- **Adds to the experience?** Yes — live upvoting is the key mechanic; it surfaces the questions the room actually wants asked, not just what one moderator picks.

## 19. Dance Floor Dedications

**Vibes:** Make them cry · Get them talking
**Moments:** Dancing

### The couple this is for
Fatima and Zach met at Fatima's cousin's wedding. Zach's grandmother literally introduced them across the dance floor. Everything about their story flows through dance.

### What the app does
A guest requests a song *and* dedicates it to someone specific in the room. When the song plays, the DJ says: *"This next one is from Alex, dedicated to her sister Rachel, who's been married 32 years and taught Alex what love was supposed to look like."* Then the song plays. Rachel probably cries. Everyone cheers.

### Cool features
- Guests can queue a dedication for anytime during dancing, not just their song
- Dedicated person gets a text: *"Ted just requested a song for you. Come to the dance floor."*
- Special *"couples' anniversary spotlight"* — the DJ can dedicate a song to the longest-married couple in the room, and pull them onto the floor
- All dedications become a printed program the couple keeps

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
The whole point of the app is *"get on the dance floor."* It creates constant micro-events that pull specific people out to dance. The phone is used for 30 seconds, and then everyone's dancing together for 3 minutes.

### Non-app version
Paper dedication slips at the DJ booth; DJ reads them aloud before the song.
- **Needed?** No — slips work.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally — auto-texting the dedicated person to come to the floor is the one genuinely-new mechanic. The rest is DJ + paper.

## 20. The Slow Reveal

**Vibes:** Get them talking · Stop the room
**Moments:** All Day

### The couple this is for
Delilah and Auggie are theater kids. Their proposal was staged. Their invitations included a fake press clipping. Every party they host has a mystery to solve, always.

### What the app does
Throughout the night, cryptic clues appear on side screens and in guests' phones. *"There is a secret in this room, and 3 people know it. By the end of the night, we hope you'll know it too."* Clues escalate. Guests can submit guesses. At midnight, the truth is revealed — and it's something real about the couple no one knew (how they *actually* got engaged, a shared secret, a hidden shared hobby).

### Cool features
- Multi-track puzzles — some solvable by everyone, some hidden behind interactions ("find the person wearing green shoes and ask about the number seven")
- The 3 "insider" guests can drop hints anonymously through the app
- Guests who solve early can join the insiders and drop hints of their own
- The final reveal is a full theatrical moment — video, lighting, the whole room hushed

### Why it respects the IRL wedding
The mystery *is* the icebreaker. Guests are forced to talk to strangers to solve clues. And the reveal itself is a shared room moment — no phones — that gives the night an ending that isn't just "the DJ played 'Closing Time.'"

### Non-app version
Printed clue cards handed out through the night, physical props hidden around the venue, an MC-run reveal at midnight.
- **Needed?** No — physical puzzle-hunts are actually more theatrical than screens.
- **Adds to the experience?** Marginally — the app enables private hint-drops from insider guests and personalized clue tracks, but for theatre-kid couples the analog version might be more on-brand. Consider hybrid.

## The pattern behind all 20

Every app on this list is:

1. **Impossible to build generically.** You can't buy "Guess The Year" off the shelf — you need the couple's 40 photos and a friend willing to sequence them. You can't launch "Songs With Stories" without the couple's "do not play" list. The customization *is* the product.
2. **Time-boxed.** No app on this list runs continuously for 8 hours demanding attention. Each has designated moments and dormant hours.
3. **A trigger for IRL emotion.** The screen event is short. The reaction happens in the room.
4. **A gift that keeps giving.** Every app produces a physical or long-term artifact — a book, a film, a scheduled email, an album. The wedding night is the launch event, not the whole product.
