# 20 Niche Custom "During the Wedding" App Ideas
*Apps so personalized that couples would pay $500–$1,000 to have one built just for them.*

The premise: a couple hears the idea, pictures it at their wedding, and says **"that would be so us."** Generic tools don't do this. These require real personalization — the couple's names, story, photos, guests, venue — baked in at build time.

---

## 1. Live "How Well Do You Know Us?" Trivia
**The pitch:** Think Kahoot, but every question is about the couple. "Where did they go on their first date?" "What did he say when he proposed?" Guests play live on their phones; a leaderboard is projected on the reception screen. The winner gives a toast.

**What makes it wow:** Guests learn things about the couple they didn't know. The couple gets to write the questions and decide what to reveal. Totally different from generic trivia.

**How it works:**
- Couple pre-fills 15–20 questions + answers + funny wrong answers (like a custom Kahoot)
- App is locked to the event (accessible via QR only that day)
- Live leaderboard on a TV/projector via second-screen URL
- Host (MC or app itself) reads questions aloud while guests answer on phones
- Optional: couple answers live too — guests see if they match

**Why $500–$1,000:** Custom question set, couple's photos on each question, branded UI with their names/colors, live sync backend, second-screen display mode.

## 2. Venue Scavenger Hunt with Their Love Story
**The pitch:** Hidden QR codes are placed around the venue (bar, photo wall, dance floor, garden). Each one unlocks a chapter of the couple's story — the story of how they met, their first trip, the proposal — told with photos and a short narrative. Guests explore and collect all chapters.

**What makes it wow:** It turns cocktail hour and the venue itself into an experience. Shy guests have something to do. It's a treasure hunt through the couple's actual life.

**How it works:**
- Couple writes 8–12 "chapters" of their story with photos
- Each chapter is a QR-linked page, placed physically at a meaningful spot ("the bar because that's where we had our first drink together")
- App tracks which chapters each guest has found
- Completing all chapters unlocks a secret message from the couple
- Optional: found-chapters leaderboard or collaborative map

**Why $500–$1,000:** Content entry interface for the couple, QR generation, chapter-tracking per guest device, final unlock mechanic, custom design.

## 3. Anniversary Time Capsule — Guest Video Messages
**The pitch:** Guests record short video messages (30–60 seconds) to be delivered to the couple on their 1st, 5th, and 10th anniversaries. The app seals the capsule at midnight. The couple can't open it — only the app unlocks it on the right date.

**What makes it wow:** Grandparents can leave a message for the couple to open when the grandparent might not be there anymore. It transforms the guest experience from celebration into something lasting and emotional.

**How it works:**
- Guests record video directly in the browser (no app install)
- They choose which anniversary to seal it for (1st, 5th, 10th, or "whenever you need it")
- App stores videos and locks them until the target date
- On the anniversary, couple receives an email with an unlock link
- Optional: couple can leave messages to each other too

**Why $500–$1,000:** Secure time-locked video storage, date-triggered email delivery, the trust factor (videos must survive years), couple dashboard for previewing what's sealed without seeing the content.

## 4. Guest Bucket List Builder
**The pitch:** During the reception, guests add items to the couple's shared life bucket list. "Go to Japan together." "Adopt a dog." "Skinny dip in the ocean." The list grows live on a display wall. The couple keeps it forever.

**What makes it wow:** It's collaborative, participatory, and forward-looking. Instead of advice (generic) it asks guests to imagine the couple's future. The output is a real artifact the couple uses.

**How it works:**
- Guests submit bucket list items via phone (text, optionally a short voice note)
- Items appear live on a projected display wall
- Couple can heart/star favorites in real time
- After the event, the couple receives a beautifully formatted PDF/digital "Bucket List" to print or frame
- Optional: guests can claim items ("I'll make this happen for you") for a twist

**Why $500–$1,000:** Real-time submission + display sync, moderation, custom branded display wall, post-event formatted export.

## 5. Table-Specific Conversation Starter Cards
**The pitch:** Each table has a unique QR code. When guests scan it, they get conversation prompts specifically generated for *that table's guest mix* — e.g., Table 3 is all college friends, so prompts are about the couple's university years. Table 7 is family with kids, so prompts include "what's your funniest memory of [bride/groom] growing up?"

**What makes it wow:** It solves the awkward "who are you and how do you know the couple?" silence with topics that are already relevant. Guests feel like the couple thought of them specifically.

**How it works:**
- Couple assigns tags to each table during setup (e.g., "college friends," "groom's family," "work colleagues")
- Couple writes a few extra context notes per table ("they all went to Lisbon together in 2019")
- App generates 8–10 custom prompts per table using those notes
- Guests can hit "next" for a new prompt
- Optional: prompts escalate in intimacy level ("getting warmer" mechanic)

**Why $500–$1,000:** Couple-facing setup interface, per-table QR codes, prompt generation (templated + custom), escalation mechanic.

## 6. The Prediction Vault
**The pitch:** Guests submit sealed predictions about the couple's future — "first child by 2027," "living in Portugal in 5 years," "still arguing about the thermostat in 2040." The predictions are sealed and revealed live on the couple's future anniversaries, with guests' names attached.

**What makes it wow:** It's funny, intimate, and creates a recurring ritual every anniversary. The couple opens predictions with friends and family and scores them. It's a party game that spans decades.

**How it works:**
- Guests submit predictions via phone during the reception
- Optional: guests bet points or rate their confidence
- Predictions are grouped by predicted date (1 year, 5 years, 10 years, 25 years)
- Each anniversary, the couple unlocks that year's predictions and can log what actually happened
- Optional: "scorecard" feature where the couple marks predictions as true/false and the "winner" is announced

**Why $500–$1,000:** Long-lived data storage, time-locked reveal mechanic, anniversary email triggers, couple's scoring interface.

## 7. "Where Were You When They Met?" Guest Memory Map
**The pitch:** Guests pin their location on an interactive world map for "where were you when the couple had their first date / got engaged / etc." and add a short memory or note. The result is a gorgeous living map of the couple's people scattered around the world.

**What makes it wow:** It makes the global reach of the couple's life visible. If the bride has friends in Tokyo, Berlin, and São Paulo, seeing those pins on a map during the reception is genuinely moving.

**How it works:**
- Couple defines 2–3 date-stamped milestones ("we had our first date on March 4, 2019 in Stockholm")
- Guests answer "where were you on that date?" with a pin on a map + short note
- Map is displayed live on the reception screen, pins appearing in real time
- Optional: animated flyover of all pins as a reception display moment
- After the event, couple receives a custom printed map poster

**Why $500–$1,000:** Interactive map with real-time pin rendering, custom milestone setup, live display mode, post-event print export.

## 8. The Live Roast Board (Moderated)
**The pitch:** Guests submit anonymous (or named) roast jokes, funny memories, or loving burns about the bride or groom. The MC or couple approves them before they appear on the display wall. The crowd votes on the funniest one. The winner gives a toast.

**What makes it wow:** It gives the funny friend a channel, replaces the awkward open-mic toast situation with something structured, and makes the whole room laugh together without anyone having to stand up uninvited.

**How it works:**
- Guests submit via phone (anonymous option available)
- Couple/MC sees a moderation queue and approves/rejects
- Approved submissions appear on the display wall with a laugh/heart reaction button
- Live vote tally — most-reacted post wins
- Optional: separate boards for bride-roast vs. groom-roast

**Why $500–$1,000:** Real-time submission and moderation queue, display wall sync, voting mechanic, anonymous vs. named option.

## 9. The Couple's Unpopular Opinions Icebreaker
**The pitch:** The couple pre-loads a set of their own unpopular opinions (e.g., "pineapple on pizza is great," "The Office is overrated," "mornings are better than evenings") and guests vote agree/disagree in real time. Results show whether the room sides with the bride or groom on each take.

**What makes it wow:** It's an immediate conversation starter. Guests who don't know each other start debating. The couple gets to be themselves. It's funny and reveals personality in a way no speech does.

**How it works:**
- Couple submits 10–15 "unpopular opinions" with their own stance pre-set
- During cocktail hour, guests vote one by one on each opinion
- Results shown as: "60% of your guests agree with [bride] that mornings are better"
- Optional: guests submit their *own* unpopular opinions and the couple reacts live

**Why $500–$1,000:** Couple setup interface, live voting with results, branded opinion display, optional couple live-reaction mechanic.

## 10. The First Dance Reveal Ballot
**The pitch:** Guests are told the couple has prepared *three* different first dances and will perform whichever one gets the most votes. Guests vote on their phones during dinner — a countdown clock ticks down. At zero, the winner is announced and the couple walks out and dances to it.

**What makes it wow:** The couple actually prepared all three (or one real one and two joke options), but the tension and anticipation during voting is electric. The whole room is invested before the dance even starts.

**How it works:**
- Three options listed with song title + a teaser description ("Option A: a waltz. Option B: something you won't expect. Option C: a full choreographed routine.")
- Live vote counter visible to guests
- Countdown timer to voting close
- Results announced on the screen with a fanfare moment
- Couple receives the vote breakdown as a keepsake

**Why $500–$1,000:** Live voting with countdown, result reveal display with animation, couple setup for options, overall event pacing mechanic.

## 11. Custom Wedding Bingo
**The pitch:** Every guest gets a unique bingo card where the squares are custom moments from *this* wedding — "someone cries during the vows," "the DJ plays ABBA," "best man trips on his speech," "flower girl steals the show." First to bingo wins a prize.

**What makes it wow:** It makes guests pay attention to everything. Even shy guests are engaged. The custom squares are hilarious because the couple wrote them — they know exactly who will cry and what the DJ will play.

**How it works:**
- Couple pre-writes 40–60 custom bingo squares specific to their wedding
- App generates a unique randomized bingo card for each guest's phone
- Guests tap squares when they happen
- App validates bingo (prevents cheating) and notifies the couple/MC when someone wins
- Optional: multiple rounds, with harder squares for later rounds

**Why $500–$1,000:** Unique per-guest card generation, real-time square validation, bingo detection logic, custom square set from couple.

## 12. The Guest Advice Oracle
**The pitch:** Instead of a generic guest book, guests answer structured prompts — "best advice for a long marriage," "a mistake to avoid," "something you wish someone had told you" — and the answers are displayed as a scrolling oracle wall. The couple can ask the oracle a question live.

**What makes it wow:** The output is genuinely useful (real advice from people who know you) and the display format makes it feel like a live installation. The couple gets a curated wisdom document after the event.

**How it works:**
- Guests choose from 5–6 prompt types and write their answer
- Responses stream onto the oracle display wall in a beautiful scrolling format
- Optional: guests mark their advice as "serious," "funny," or "hard-won"
- Couple can post a live question ("we can't agree on where to live — help") and guests respond
- Post-event: formatted PDF "Book of Advice" with all responses

**Why $500–$1,000:** Real-time display wall, structured prompt UI, post-event PDF export, couple live-question mechanic.

## 13. The Relationship Origin Story — Interactive Exhibit
**The pitch:** A rich, beautifully designed digital exhibit of the couple's relationship — photos, maps, dates, milestones — that guests explore on their phones like a mini-museum. Narrated by the couple in short voice notes. It's their Wikipedia page, but gorgeous.

**What makes it wow:** Guests who don't know the full story (the groom's work colleagues, distant relatives) get genuinely drawn in. It's the "how did you two meet?" story told properly, with photos.

**How it works:**
- Couple fills in a structured content form: key dates, photos, short captions, optional voice notes
- App renders it as a scrollable visual timeline / exhibit
- Unlocks during cocktail hour via QR code at the entrance
- Guests can leave a reaction ("this made me cry") on each chapter
- Optional: a hidden "Easter egg" chapter only findable by guests who read closely

**Why $500–$1,000:** Content entry interface, beautiful mobile-first exhibit renderer, voice note integration, reaction tracking, Easter egg mechanic.

## 14. "Design Our Future Home" Collaborative Map
**The pitch:** An interactive map of the city (or world) where guests pin places meaningful to the couple or places they think the couple should move to / visit. Each pin has a short note. The map fills up live during the reception.

**What makes it wow:** It's visual, grows in real time on a display, and is something the couple actually uses after (it becomes a living bucket-list map of places to go together).

**How it works:**
- Couple pre-pins a few "our places" markers (apartment, where they had their first date, proposal spot)
- Guests add pins with short notes ("you should eat here," "move to this neighborhood," "this park will be perfect for your kids")
- Map displayed live at the reception, zooming in as pins cluster
- After the event, couple receives an interactive version and optional printed poster
- Optional: pins are color-coded by guest relationship type

**Why $500–$1,000:** Real-time map pin sync, display mode with live zoom, couple's pre-pinned layer, post-event static export.

## 15. The Couple's Soundtrack — Collaborative Playlist with Stories
**The pitch:** Guests don't just request songs — they submit a song *and* the memory it represents ("Dancing Queen — this played in your car on the way to the lake house and you both screamed every word"). The DJ plays the top-voted songs; the stories stream on the display wall as each song plays.

**What makes it wow:** It transforms song requests into a shared narrative. The moment "Dancing Queen" plays and that memory appears on the screen, everyone at that lake house table loses it.

**How it works:**
- Guests submit: song title + 1–2 sentence memory or reason
- Voting bumps songs up the queue
- DJ receives an approved playlist queue in real time
- As each song starts, its submitted memory is displayed on the wall
- Optional: guests can add new memories mid-song ("I remember this too — it was also playing when…")

**Why $500–$1,000:** Submission + voting + queue management + DJ display interface + live wall renderer — more complex than a simple request app.

## 16. The "Unprompted Love Letter" Machine
**The pitch:** Guests submit a secret message, memory, or love note addressed to the bride, the groom, or both. Messages are anonymous by default. At a chosen moment (after dinner, before speeches), the couple sits together and reads them live — on screen, one by one — for the first time.

**What makes it wow:** The raw authenticity of reading unscripted messages from people you love, in real time, in front of everyone, is genuinely emotional. It's the moment guests most want to witness.

**How it works:**
- Guests submit messages during cocktail hour and dinner (anonymous option)
- Couple/MC can preview in moderation queue and mark ones for the live reading moment
- At the designated moment, messages appear on screen one by one (couple controls pace)
- Guests who wrote each message can "reveal" themselves afterward if they want
- Post-event: couple receives all messages in a formatted keepsake document

**Why $500–$1,000:** Moderation queue, timed reveal interface for the couple, display wall sync, reveal-identity mechanic, post-event export.

## 17. Wedding Day Emotion Pulse
**The pitch:** Throughout the day, guests get a gentle notification asking "how are you feeling right now?" with a simple emotion picker. The aggregate emotion is displayed as a live "pulse" visualization on the reception screen. Peaks during the vows, first dance, and speeches — captured forever.

**What makes it wow:** It turns the crowd's collective feeling into a visible, real-time artifact. Looking at the emotion graph afterward and seeing the spike during the vows is unexpectedly moving.

**How it works:**
- Guests opt into pulse notifications when they scan the event QR
- Brief prompts arrive 5–6 times during the day (ceremony, cocktail hour, dinner, dancing)
- Response is a simple emoji / emotion scale (takes 2 seconds)
- Live visualization on display screen shows the crowd's emotional state in real time
- Post-event: couple receives a "day in feelings" graph as a keepsake

**Why $500–$1,000:** Push notification scheduling, real-time aggregate visualization, timed prompt logic across ceremony phases, post-event infographic export.

## 18. Table-to-Table Secret Relay
**The pitch:** A message relay game where each table writes a secret message or dare that gets passed to the next table — who must complete it, photograph the result, and pass a new one on. It's telephone meets truth-or-dare, and the results are displayed on the wall.

**What makes it wow:** It connects tables that don't know each other. By the end of cocktail hour, Table 4 has a photo of themselves completing a dare from Table 1 and everyone is laughing.

**How it works:**
- Each table submits a message/dare/challenge for the next table
- App routes messages in sequence (or randomly)
- Receiving table reads their message, completes it, and uploads a photo as proof
- Completed challenges appear on the display wall
- Optional: the couple assigns the routing (bride's college friends dare the groom's work colleagues)

**Why $500–$1,000:** Per-table routing logic, photo upload mechanic, display wall renderer, couple's routing control.

## 19. The Personalized Cocktail Quiz
**The pitch:** Guests answer a short personality/preference quiz ("are you more of a morning or a night person?", "city or countryside?") and the app recommends a personalized cocktail or mocktail from the wedding's custom drink menu — named after moments in the couple's relationship.

**What makes it wow:** The couple's custom drink menu has names like "The First Date Fizz" and "The Proposal Punch" — the quiz matches you to one. It's a conversation starter, a fun distraction during cocktail hour, and a way for the couple to share their story through the menu.

**How it works:**
- Couple creates the drink menu with names + short lore ("we had this cocktail the night he proposed in Lisbon")
- Couple maps quiz outcomes to drinks
- Guests take the quiz on arrival; app shows their recommended drink with the story behind it
- Guests can share their result ("I got The Proposal Punch") on a social wall
- Optional: bartenders see a live dashboard of what's trending

**Why $500–$1,000:** Quiz builder + drink mapping interface, result display with drink lore, social sharing wall, bartender dashboard.

## 20. The "If You Hadn't Met" Parallel Universe Game
**The pitch:** Guests imagine and submit an alternate-universe scenario — what would have happened to the bride and groom if they had *never* met? Funny, absurd, or poignant — all work. The couple reads the best ones aloud during dinner, then reveals the truth of how close they came to never meeting.

**What makes it wow:** It's a creative icebreaker that makes guests think deeply about the couple's story. The best entries are usually hilarious ("he'd have married his college roommate's ex") or genuinely touching ("she'd have moved to New York and been a completely different person"). And the couple's real "almost didn't meet" story is usually more dramatic than anyone guesses.

**How it works:**
- Guests submit a parallel universe scenario (capped at 150 characters — forces creativity)
- Couple and MC review submissions and pick favorites for live reading
- Selected submissions appear on the display wall one by one
- The couple then reveals the real "how we almost didn't meet" story
- Post-event: all submissions collected in a keepsake document

**Why $500–$1,000:** Submission + moderation interface, character-limited input, display wall with reveal animation, couple's storytelling moment interface.

## What These Have in Common

All 20 share a pattern worth noting when building:

| Pattern | Why it matters |
|---------|---------------|
| **Couple-authored content** | The app is empty without the couple filling it in — which is fine; the setup is part of the fun |
| **Live display component** | Almost all have a second-screen "wall" element — a projector URL that non-technical couples can just open in a browser |
| **No app install for guests** | Guests access everything via QR → mobile browser |
| **A keepsake output** | The experience produces something the couple keeps (PDF, video, map, graph) — that's part of the $500–$1,000 value |
| **Moderation built in** | Couples need a way to filter before anything goes on the wall — always include this |
| **Works in 2–4 hours** | The app is used during a window; it doesn't need to scale or persist forever |
