# Landing Page v2 — Copy Suggestions

Based on the reinvented UVP in `zz/one-pager.md` and the 20 apps in `zz/info/20-apps-reimagined.md`.

**The three ideas the LP has to land, in order:**
1. These apps are **built from scratch for your specific couple** — you can even bring your own idea
2. They **respect the IRL wedding** — phone-in/phone-out, no scroll hole, they *cause* real-world moments
3. They are a **great-value add** — a keepsake that outlives the flowers and the caterer

Every copy change below serves one of those three.

---

## Section-by-section

### 1. `HomeHero`

**Current**
- Eyebrow: *Custom Wedding Experience Studio*
- H1: *A wedding app built around who you actually are.*
- Sub: *Not a template. Not Kahoot. Built for your wedding, from scratch. Price $1,000–2,000*
- CTAs: *Explore 20 example apps →* / *Book your app*

**Suggested**
- Eyebrow: *Custom Wedding Experience Studio* (keep)
- H1: **A wedding app built for one couple. Yours.**
  - *Alt A:* *An app built for your wedding — and no one else's, ever.*
  - *Alt B:* *The only wedding app that knows how you met.*
- Sub: **Bring us an idea, or pick one of ours. We build it from scratch — designed for your wedding, seamless on the night, phone-in and phone-out in under a minute. ~$2,000.**
- Primary CTA: *See 20 things we've built* (softer than "explore 20 example apps" — implies real work, not a catalog)
- Secondary CTA: *Talk to us about yours →* (invites the "bring your own idea" behaviour rule 4 unlocks)

**Why:** the current hero sells "custom" but not "custom *to what degree*." The word **one** does more work than "custom" ever will. The sub swaps "not a template, not Kahoot" (defensive framing) for the two active promises: *you can bring an idea* + *we make it feel like part of the wedding.*

### 6. `HowItWorks` — Three steps

The current three steps don't reflect the new UVP. Step 2 says *"you write the trivia questions"* — that assumes a template. Under rule 4/5, the flow is different.

**Suggested rewrite (still three steps):**

**01 — Bring an idea, or pick one of ours**
*Twenty apps we've built are on this site — pick one and we'll make it about you. Or bring us something no one has ever done at a wedding before. Weird, specific, tied to a private inside joke — if it fits your story, we'll build it.*

**02 — We design and build it (2–4 weeks)**
*You tell us the stories, share the photos, name the moments. We handle the code, the design, the wedding-day reliability. It arrives looking like part of your wedding — not a bolt-on tool.*

**03 — QR on every table. That's it.**
*Guests scan. No download, no login, no app store. Your grandmother can do it. Your most technophobic uncle can do it. We're on call during the reception if anything wobbles.*

**Why:** step 1 now activates rule 4 (bring your own idea). Step 2 activates rule 5 (we do the work, wedding-native design). Step 3 keeps the "no download" reassurance from the current step 3 but tightens it.

**Section subheading:** change *"Three steps. One extraordinary night."* → **"Three steps. Zero templates."**

### 7. New section (recommended) — *"The Five Rules"*

Insert between `HowItWorks` and `PlannersCallout`. This is the single biggest UVP payload on the page.

**Heading:** *What every Wepho app respects*

**Layout:** 5 cards, 2–3 columns, one line each.

1. **Phone in, phone out in under 60 seconds.** No feed. No scroll hole. Guests interact, then look up.
2. **The magic happens in the room, not the screen.** Every app triggers something real — a toast, a hug, a burst of laughter from table three.
3. **You curate everything.** Nothing hits the screen without your (or a trusted friend's) approval. No awkward surprises.
4. **Built for one couple. Yours.** Nothing off the shelf. Bring us an idea — however weird, however private, however specific — and if it fits your story, we build it.
5. **You bring the idea. We build it beautifully.** You don't touch code, wireframes, or config. We ship it with wedding-native design and rock-solid live-night reliability.

**Why this section matters more than any other change:** rules 1–3 answer *"won't this ruin the vibe"* (biggest objection). Rules 4–5 answer *"is this actually for us"* and *"do we have to do work?"* (next two biggest). It's the whole pitch on one screen.

## Micro-copy: the price frame

Wherever price appears on the LP, standardize to a single sentence that lands the value:

> **~$2,000. Every guest is in. You keep it forever. They talk about it for years.**

Or, borrowing from the one-pager comparison, use it as a proof-line under the hero sub or above the contact form:

> *Flowers: $800, dead by morning. Photographer: $3,500, seen in 6 weeks. Wepho: ~$2,000, remembered forever.*

Currently the hero sub shows *"Price $1,000–2,000"* as an afterthought — move it out of the hero (weakens H1) and into the FinalCta as reassurance where price objections actually surface.

---

## Summary of changes to make first (if only doing 3)

1. **Hero H1 + sub:** land "one couple, yours" + "bring your own idea" upfront.
2. **New "Five Rules" section** between HowItWorks and PlannersCallout — the single highest-leverage addition on the page.
3. **HowItWorks rewrite:** step 1 becomes "bring an idea or pick one of ours"; step 2 becomes "we design and build it."

Everything else is polish. These three shift the whole page from *"we made 20 templates"* to *"we build one thing, for you, from scratch."*
