# Wepho Pinterest Marketing Plan

## 1) Research how Pinterest actually works

**The core mental model:** Pinterest is a visual search engine with a long content half-life, not a social feed. A pin you post today can drive traffic 8–12 months from now. Success is driven by keywords + fresh visuals + consistency, not by follower count or virality.

**Research steps:**
- Set up a **Business account** (not personal). This unlocks analytics, Rich Pins, and ad tools — non-negotiable.
- Verify the wepho.com domain and enable **Rich Pins** so pins auto-sync your page title/description.
- Study how the **algorithm ranks**: it rewards (a) keyword relevance in pin title/description/board, (b) *fresh* content — new image + new copy each time, and (c) steady daily activity. Repinning the same image to many boards is now flagged as spam and suppresses reach.
- Reverse-engineer the search itself: type "wedding reception ideas", "unique wedding entertainment", "wedding guest activities" into the search bar and watch the **autocomplete suggestions** and the colored **keyword tiles** that appear under the bar — those are Pinterest telling you exactly what real people search.
- Read 2–3 current guides to calibrate: [Hootsuite's 2026 guide](https://blog.hootsuite.com/pinterest-marketing/), [Sprout Social small-business guide](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/pinterest-for-small-business-marketing/), and the wedding-specific angle from [Pin with Paula](https://www.pinwithpaula.com/blog/wedding-photographers-what-should-you-actually-do-for-marketing-in-2026).

**Deliverable:** a one-page "how Pinterest ranks us" cheat sheet + a keyword seed list of 30–50 phrases from autocomplete.

## 2) Understand who's really on there

Pinterest's wedding audience is **overwhelmingly the couple in active planning mode** (and their planners/MOHs), searching intentionally rather than scrolling. That's Wepho's exact ICP.

**Research steps:**
- Use **Pinterest Trends** (trends.pinterest.com) to see search volume and seasonality for wedding terms — wedding planning searches spike in a predictable annual curve (engagement season Dec–Feb → planning surge spring/summer).
- Use **Audience Insights** in the business dashboard once you have activity: it shows age, gender, location, and *other categories* your audience engages with (e.g. your audience may also follow "wedding DIY", "reception decor", "party games").
- Build 2–3 **audience personas** from this: e.g. "Emma, 27, engaged 4 months, planning a 120-guest reception, saving 'unique reception entertainment' pins"; and a **planner persona** who curates boards for multiple clients.
- Distinguish the two buyer types (couples vs. planners) — they search different terms and want different pins. Planners search "wedding vendor ideas", "client reception timeline"; couples search "fun things for guests to do at wedding".

**Deliverable:** 2–3 personas + the actual search phrases each uses.

## 3) Plan content & interactions (best practices)

**Content strategy — organize around SEO and the two demos:**
- **Boards as landing zones.** Create keyword-named boards, not clever ones: "Interactive Wedding Reception Ideas", "Fun Wedding Guest Activities", "Unique Wedding Entertainment", "Wedding Big-Screen Moments", plus a location board if you target a region ("[City] Wedding Ideas"). Give each board a keyword-rich description.
- **Every pin links back** to a specific Wepho page — homepage demo, `/apps`, or an individual `/apps/[slug]` page. The `/apps/[slug]` pages are your SEO-primary destinations, so pin heavily to those.
- **Fresh pins, not repins.** For one app (say Love Letter Machine), design 5–8 *visually distinct* pins with different images and different keyword-led copy. This is the sanctioned way to get volume without triggering spam filters.
- **Show the moment, not the product.** Pinterest rewards aspirational scenes: a guest typing on their phone, a heartfelt message revealed on the big screen, the room reacting. Wepho's phone-mockup + big-screen visuals are natively pinnable.
- **Copy formula:** keyword-rich title + 100–300 char description with primary + secondary keywords written naturally (no stuffing) + a soft CTA ("See how it works").

**Cadence & interaction:**
- **Consistency beats bursts** — a few fresh pins daily/most-days over months. Use a scheduler (Tailwind or Pinterest's native scheduler) so it's sustainable.
- **Engage vendor-to-vendor**, not with random users: comment on and save pins from planners, venues, and photographers; join or create **group/collaborative boards** to tap their audiences.
- Set realistic expectations: **6–12+ months to meaningful traffic.** Track leading indicators (saves, outbound clicks) early rather than bookings.

**Deliverable:** a content calendar template + a per-app "pin pack" spec (8 pin variations each).

## 4) Understand analytics

Pinterest's own metrics matter more than followers (a supporting metric only). Focus on the funnel:

| Metric              | What it means                        | Why it matters to Wepho                                                                                             |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Impressions**     | Times a pin appeared (feed/search)   | Top-of-funnel reach — is Pinterest showing us?                                                                      |
| **Saves**           | User saved your pin to their board   | The strongest signal — expands distribution *and* tells the algorithm to show it more. Optimize creative for saves. |
| **Pin clicks**      | Clicks to expand the pin             | Interest level in the pin itself                                                                                    |
| **Outbound clicks** | Clicks through to wepho.com          | **The real KPI** — this is traffic to your booking pages                                                            |
| **Engagements**     | Saves + pin clicks + outbound clicks | Overall pull                                                                                                        |

**Research steps:**
- Learn the native dashboard: [Pinterest's analytics help doc](https://help.pinterest.com/en/business/article/pinterest-analytics) and [pin stats guide](https://help.pinterest.com/en/business/article/pin-stats). Sort Top Pins by *outbound clicks* and by *saves* to see what's working.
- Close the loop with **Google Analytics / Vercel Analytics** on the site — track Pinterest as a referral source and see which `/apps/[slug]` pages convert to contact-form submissions. Pinterest's outbound clicks + your site's conversion data = true ROI.
- Set a monthly review: which pins earn saves, which boards drive outbound clicks, then make *more variations of the winners* and retire duds.

**Deliverable:** a simple monthly analytics review template (top 5 pins by save + by outbound click, + site conversions from Pinterest referral).

## 5) Post formats available

All pins are now vertical-first. As of 2026 the formats unified — every pin can hold image, video, or both.

- **Standard image pin** — **1000×1500px, 2:3 ratio** (the workhorse). JPG/PNG/WEBP, up to ~20MB. Anything taller than 1:2.1 gets cropped. *Use for:* app showcase pins, big-screen-moment stills, "6 apps" grids.
- **Video pin** — **6–15 sec, hook in first 2–3 sec** (autoplays muted, so open on the visual + text overlay). 9:16 or 2:3 at 1080p+ gets the most feed visibility. *Use for:* a 10-sec loop of a guest typing → message appearing on the big screen. High algorithmic priority right now.
- **Idea/multi-page pin** — up to 20 pages, **1080×1920 (9:16)**; keep text out of the top ~180px and bottom ~260px (UI overlays). *Use for:* "How the Love Letter Machine works, step by step" or "5 of our 20 apps".
- **Product/Rich pins** — auto-pull metadata from your linked page; enable these so pins stay accurate.
- **Promoted pins (ads)** — paid amplification once you know which organic pins convert. Don't start here; start organic, then boost proven winners.

Specs sourced from [Pinterest pin size guide](https://postfa.st/sizes/pinterest/pin) and [image sizes 2026](https://posteverywhere.ai/blog/pinterest-image-sizes).

**Deliverable:** a template set (Figma/Canva) at 1000×1500 and 1080×1920 matching Wepho's design tokens.

## 6) Accounts to follow & interact with for exposure

Interact **laterally with the wedding-vendor ecosystem** — their audiences *are* your buyers, and collaboration (group boards, saves, comments) is Pinterest's real growth lever.

**Categories to find and engage (search + follow 10–15 of each):**
- **Wedding planners & coordinators** — especially in your target region; they're both a buyer segment *and* a distribution channel. Prioritize these.
- **Wedding photographers** — huge, active pinners with couple audiences; ideal collab-board partners.
- **Venues & event spaces** — followed by couples who've just booked and are now planning the experience.
- **Big wedding publications/aggregators** — Green Wedding Shoes, Style Me Pretty, Junebug Weddings, The Knot, Brides, Martha Stewart Weddings, WeddingWire — study their board structure and pin cadence; getting a pin saved by one is outsized reach.
- **Reception-entertainment & wedding-DIY accounts** — closest content-adjacency to what Wepho sells.

**How to interact (not spam):**
- Follow, then genuinely **save their relevant pins to your boards** and leave specific comments.
- Pitch **collaborative boards** with 3–5 aligned planners/photographers ("Fun Reception Ideas" shared board).
- Explore **vendor cross-promotion** — you pin their work, they pin yours; taps their existing audience directly (the single most-recommended tactic in the wedding-Pinterest guides, e.g. [Julia Renee Consulting](https://juliareneeconsulting.com/pinterest-strategies-for-wedding-photographers/)).

**Deliverable:** a living list of 40–60 target accounts (name, type, region, collab potential) + an outreach shortlist for group boards.
